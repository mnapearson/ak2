Born to a working-class family in the Bronx, New York, I gained firsthand knowledge of political disenfranchisement in America. This consciousness was molded by a family with strong ties to human-rights advocacy. My mother Iseult Keith, PhD, was a social worker in Sing Sing prison, providing counseling to the incarcerated. My father, William “Bill” Keith, was a poet, teacher, Civil-Rights activist, and ally of Malcolm X, as well as a Black Arts Movement avant-garde artist. He was also a two-time victim of predatory policing and mass incarceration.

Their story inspired a keen understanding that those with the greatest proximity to pain should be closest to power. As Bill and Iseult’s son, I aim to build on their legacy through progressive action on the policy level. I do so by engaging with electoral politics as well as educational and corporate institutions and networking at the global level with wide-ranging anti-discrimination projects.
